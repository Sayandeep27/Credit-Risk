## 1. What is **Credit Risk?**

**Credit Risk** is the **risk that a borrower will not repay the money they borrowed**.

In simple words:

> **Credit Risk = Risk of losing money because the borrower does not pay back the loan.**

This is very important for **banks, lenders, credit card companies, and financial institutions**.

### Simple Example

A bank gives **₹1,00,000 loan** to a person.

Two possibilities:

| Situation             | Result              |
| --------------------- | ------------------- |
| Person repays loan    | Bank earns interest |
| Person does NOT repay | Bank loses money    |

The possibility of the **second situation** is called **Credit Risk**.

---

# 2. Why Credit Risk is Important

Banks lend money to **thousands or millions of people**.

If many borrowers **fail to repay**, the bank can lose huge money.

Therefore banks must:

* Evaluate borrower risk
* Decide **whether to give a loan**
* Decide **interest rate**
* Decide **loan amount**

Higher risk → **Higher interest rate**.

---

# 3. Where Credit Risk Happens

Credit risk appears in many financial products.

| Product        | Example                          |
| -------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Loans          | Home loan, personal loan         |
| Credit cards   | Customer doesn't pay credit bill |
| Bonds          | Company cannot repay bondholders |
| Business loans | Company goes bankrupt            |

Example:

If a company borrows **₹10 crore** and becomes bankrupt → lenders lose money.

That loss comes from **credit risk**.

---

# 4. How Credit Risk is Calculated

Financial institutions usually calculate credit risk using **three important components**.

### Credit Risk Formula

```
Credit Risk = PD × LGD × EAD
```

Where:

| Term    | Meaning                |
| ------- | ---------------------- |
| **PD**  | Probability of Default |
| **LGD** | Loss Given Default     |
| **EAD** | Exposure at Default    |

---

# 5. Probability of Default (PD)

**PD = Probability that the borrower will fail to repay the loan.**

Value is between **0 and 1** (or 0% to 100%).

Example:

| Borrower        | PD  |
| --------------- | --- |
| Good borrower   | 2%  |
| Medium borrower | 10% |
| Risky borrower  | 30% |

If **PD = 10%**

It means:

> There is a **10% chance the borrower will default**.

Banks estimate PD using:

* Credit score
* Income
* past loan history
* employment stability
* debt level

---

# 6. Loss Given Default (LGD)

**LGD = How much money the bank loses if the borrower defaults.**

Formula:

```
LGD = Loss / Total Loan
```

Example:

Loan = **₹1,00,000**

Borrower defaults but bank recovers **₹40,000** from assets.

Loss = **₹60,000**

```
LGD = 60,000 / 1,00,000 = 0.6
```

So

**LGD = 60%**

---

# 7. Exposure at Default (EAD)

**EAD = Total amount owed by borrower at the time of default.**

Example:

Loan sanctioned = **₹1,00,000**

Borrower has already repaid **₹20,000**

Remaining loan = **₹80,000**

So:

**EAD = ₹80,000**

---

# 8. Complete Credit Risk Example

Suppose a bank gives a **₹1,00,000 loan**.

Assume:

| Component | Value     |
| --------- | --------- |
| PD        | 10%       |
| LGD       | 60%       |
| EAD       | ₹1,00,000 |

Now apply formula:

```
Credit Risk = PD × LGD × EAD
```

```
= 0.10 × 0.60 × 100000
```

```
= 6000
```

### Final Result

**Expected Loss = ₹6,000**

Meaning:

> On average the bank expects to lose **₹6,000 from this loan**.

The **5 C’s of Credit** are a **framework used by banks and lenders to evaluate whether a borrower is reliable enough to receive a loan**.
It helps lenders **assess credit risk before giving money**.

In simple terms:

> The **5 C’s of Credit = Five factors banks check before approving a loan.**

These five factors are:

1. **Character**
2. **Capacity**
3. **Capital**
4. **Collateral**
5. **Conditions**

---

# 1. Character

**Character means the borrower’s trustworthiness and repayment behavior.**

Banks try to answer:

> **Is this person responsible enough to repay the loan?**

They evaluate:

* Credit history
* Past loan repayment behavior
* Credit score (like **CIBIL score**)
* Payment history of credit cards

### Example

Person A:

* Pays credit card bills on time
* Has good credit history
* CIBIL score = **780**

Person B:

* Missed many payments
* Has loan defaults
* CIBIL score = **520**

Bank conclusion:

| Borrower | Risk      |
| -------- | --------- |
| Person A | Low risk  |
| Person B | High risk |

So **Person A has better Character**.

---

# 2. Capacity

**Capacity means the borrower’s ability to repay the loan.**

Banks analyze whether the borrower **earns enough money to repay the loan**.

They check:

* Monthly income
* Existing loans
* Job stability
* Debt-to-income ratio

### Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)

```
DTI = Total Monthly Debt / Monthly Income
```

### Example

Income = ₹60,000 per month
Existing EMI = ₹10,000

```
DTI = 10,000 / 60,000 = 16.7%
```

Lower DTI → safer borrower.

Typical bank rule:

| DTI    | Risk     |
| ------ | -------- |
| < 30%  | Good     |
| 30–40% | Moderate |
| > 40%  | Risky    |

---

# 3. Capital

**Capital means how much personal money the borrower has invested.**

Banks prefer borrowers who **have their own money in the investment**.

Why?

Because they are **less likely to default**.

### Example: Home Loan

House price = ₹50 lakh

| Borrower contribution | Bank loan |
| --------------------- | --------- |
| ₹10 lakh              | ₹40 lakh  |

Borrower invested **₹10 lakh from own savings**.

This shows **financial commitment**.

Higher capital → lower risk.

---

# 4. Collateral

**Collateral is an asset pledged to secure the loan.**

If the borrower fails to repay, the bank **seizes the asset and sells it**.

Common collateral:

* House
* Land
* Car
* Gold
* Business assets

### Example

Loan = ₹5 lakh
Collateral = Car worth ₹6 lakh

If borrower defaults:

Bank sells car → recovers money.

Collateral **reduces credit risk**.

---

# 5. Conditions

**Conditions refer to the purpose of the loan and economic environment.**

Banks evaluate:

* Why the loan is needed
* Industry conditions
* Economic situation
* Interest rate environment

### Example

Two business loan requests:

| Business                 | Bank view   |
| ------------------------ | ----------- |
| Renewable energy company | Good future |
| Failing textile company  | Risky       |

Economic conditions also matter.

Example:

During **recession**, banks become **more strict** in lending.
